shedding (2024) - Kathleen Egan & Inflationary Assets

Curated by Johannes Birringer at Gallery Puzić

.

reception

The piece was a success! With a rough attendance of 35-40 people in person and an undetermined number on the live stream, it had a pretty good attendance with some great audience feedback. We had a really mixed audience who, for the most part, happily got involved joining in with the cutting and found the piece to be mediative and beautiful with the way it moved.

It lasted just a fraction under time, ending after approximately 170 minutes, before hanging in the space for an additional hour. It's always hard to judge perofrmance works with how long they'll end up lasting but apparently the work could have gone on longer.

Part way through I had a moment of realisation that the main draw of the piece was as an evolving sculptural work, thats best witnessed in small repeated instances over its run time. It complimented the gallery it was set in fairly well.

.

considerations

Agnes did an incredible job on the dresses design. From what started as me fumbling with fabric the Sunday before the show, she marshelled it into a beautiful structure, that looked just phenomenal in the space. Additionally the dress packed down so easily for transport. Next time I promise to give you more time!

The cloth was a struggle to hang due to the galleries high ceilings and a lack of infrastructure to support the weight. In the end we decided on a rigging system to suspend the piece freely allowing it to move with me during the performance.

Once it was up, it flowed beautifully with audience members comparing it to the movement of a sail in the wind. It twisted gorgeously around my form as I moved and made for a perfect performance tool.

.

theoretics

The piece is an obvious homage to the work of Yoko Ono's Cut Piece but with an inverse of thematic direction. With Cut Piece, the clothes were an armour and a sense of safety that were being stripped away, the audience were clawing for a sense of the imtimacy with the artist, a wish to truely know her. With shedding, I was looking to do the inverse. I wanted to invite the audience to free me, from a prison of cloth.

At a few moments, I had a realisation that what I'd created for myself was more of a panopticon, where at all moments I was being obveserved but through which I was totally unable to see more than silhouettes of my audience.

My mental state in the structure was fragile, all I could hear was sounds; words, movement, camera shutters, movement of cloth, cars passing the open door of the gallery and breathing. It was highlighted with a self concious panic at times. I felt isolated from the room, while also its focal point.
