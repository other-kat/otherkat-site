shedding (2024) - Kathleen Egan & Inflationary Assets

Curated by Johannes Birringer at Gallery Puzić

.

To start the cloth was a nightmare to hang. We spent the best part of 3 hours getting it up and creating a rigging system that allowed it to move more freely within the space.

.

The piece is an obvious homage to the work of Yoko Ono's Cut Piece but with an inverse of thematic direction. With Cut Piece, the clothes were an armour and a sense of safety that were being stripped away, the audience were clawing for a sense of the imtimacy with the artist, a wish to truely know her. With shedding, I was looking to do the inverse. I wanted to invite the audience to free me, from a prison of cloth.

.
